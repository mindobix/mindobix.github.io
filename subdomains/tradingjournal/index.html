Frequently Asked

Common Questions

If your question isn't here, open an issue on GitHub or email ganesh@mindobix.com.

Is it really free? What's the catch?

No catch. It's open source under the MIT license, runs entirely in your browser, and there is no backend to charge you for. Mindobix builds tools we use ourselves and shares them — that's the whole model.

Where is my trade data stored?

In your browser's IndexedDB — a database built into Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. The data never leaves your machine unless you explicitly export it. No server, no cloud sync, no telemetry.

How do I move from Tradezella?

Export your trades from Tradezella as a CSV. The Trading Journal's Tradezella import auto-splits each row into open and close legs, detects short stock trades, and converts your mistake tags. Most users finish a full migration in under five minutes.

Can I sync across devices?

Not automatically — that would require a server. You can export your full database to a file and import it on another machine. Many users keep that file in their own Dropbox, iCloud, or Syncthing folder.

How does the Signal News feed work?

An optional local background server (also free, also yours) aggregates RSS news and pipes it through an LLM you control (cloud or local Ollama). Setup instructions are in the GitHub README.

Will my data survive a browser update?

Yes. IndexedDB persists across browser updates, restarts, and reboots. The only ways to lose it are: clearing browser data manually, uninstalling the browser, or your disk failing. Export regularly if your data matters.

Can I contribute or request features?

Absolutely. Open an issue or PR at github.com/mindobix/local-trading-journal. New features ship fast — this codebase is maintained by Claude Code AI Agents directed by 32 years of engineering experience.
