The New Economics of Software

Why We Don't Charge by the Hour

Hourly billing was invented for a world where developers typed every character of code. The hours spent was a reasonable proxy for value delivered.

That world is gone. AI Agents write code at machine speed. What matters now is the quality of thinking, the depth of domain knowledge, and the clarity of direction — not the number of hours a cursor blinked.

Charging by the hour when AI handles the keystrokes would mean charging you for the wrong thing. We charge for outcomes, not clocks. You should expect faster delivery and better pricing than you have ever received from a software consultant.
